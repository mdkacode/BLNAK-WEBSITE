The number comes from a 2023 study across 2,000 smartphone users: the average adult unlocks their
phone **144 times per day**. If you sleep seven hours, that is one unlock every six minutes you are
awake. You are not using your phone 144 times because 144 things happened. You are using it because
the habit of checking outruns the reason to check.

Every unlock costs two things people rarely measure. The first is **attentional residue** — the
mental debris a task leaves behind when you interrupt it. Research by Sophie Leroy shows that after
a brief switch, a measurable fraction of your focus stays with the previous task for up to 25
minutes. 144 unlocks is not 144 quick glances; it is a day sliced into paper-thin strips of
concentration.

The second cost is **anticipatory reward conditioning**. Your brain learns that unlocking sometimes
produces a dopamine hit — a message, a like, a new email — and sometimes produces nothing. That
intermittent schedule is, unfortunately, the most addictive reinforcement pattern known to
behavioral psychology. It is the same math that powers slot machines.

> It is the same math that powers slot machines — and it runs inside your pocket, all day, for free.

## The launcher is the leverage point

You cannot fix this with willpower. You have already tried. The leverage point is the interface
you see the instant your phone unlocks. If that interface is a colorful grid of app icons, each
one a little nominee for your attention, you will tap one. If it is a blank screen with a search
bar, you have to *name* what you want — which means you have to *know* what you want — before you
can get it.

That three-second pause is the entire intervention. It is not about blocking apps. It is about
forcing your prefrontal cortex to speak before your thumb does.

A minimalist launcher like BLNAK will not make you a monk. It will not add an app you didn't
already have. All it does is replace the slot machine with a question: *what did you pick up your
phone for?* Most of the time, the honest answer is *nothing*. And when the answer is nothing, you
put the phone back down. 144 unlocks becomes 40. Forty becomes a life you recognize again.
